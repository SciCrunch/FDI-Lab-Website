What is FAIR?

FAIR principles guide scientific data management and stewardship so that data can be maximally reused by humans and machines alike.

F

Findable

Data and metadata are assigned persistent, globally unique identifiers and are indexed in searchable resources.

A

Accessible

Data are retrievable by their identifier using open, standardized protocols with clear access conditions.

I

Interoperable

Data use formal, accessible, shared, and broadly applicable language for knowledge representation.

R

Reusable

Data have rich metadata, a clear license, and meet domain-relevant community standards to support replication.

About FDI Lab

Our Mission

We develop technology, services, and policy to improve accuracy, reproducibility, and analysis in biomedical research. Our work spans data portals, ontologies, persistent identifiers, and cross-database search infrastructure.

Our History

Founded in 2008 as the Neuroscience Information Framework (NIF), an NIH Blueprint Consortium initiative, FDI Lab expanded into a multi-domain platform powering portals across digestive, diabetes, kidney, spinal cord, and brain research.
